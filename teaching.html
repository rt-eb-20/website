Teaching

Six years of university teaching — at the Chair of E-Business, Martin-Luther-University Halle-Wittenberg, and at Hochschule Merseburg.

From 2020 to 2026 I independently prepared and taught Bachelor's and Master's courses, designed and marked examinations, and supervised seminar papers and theses. Two subjects sat at the core of my teaching in Halle: e-business and internet economics. Both build directly on the textbooks of my supervisor, Prof. Dr. Ralf Peters, which form the foundation of the chair's curriculum. In parallel I designed and taught two data science modules of my own at Hochschule Merseburg.

Core subjects

Bachelor

E-Business

Technologies and applications of electronic business — from digital value creation and business models to the IT instruments behind them.

Foundation: Ralf Peters, E-Business: Technologien und Anwendungen (2021, ISBN 979-8-7144-7512-2).

Master

Internet Economics

How the internet creates new digital markets and reshapes existing ones — classical economic models applied to the net, plus new ones, with cases like Amazon, eBay, and Google.

Foundation: Ralf Peters, Internet-Ökonomie (Springer, 2010, DOI, ISBN 978-3-642-10651-4).

Further teaching

Beyond the two core subjects, I taught and supervised across neighbouring topics of the chair:

  • Internet economics
  • E-business
  • IT security
  • Web engineering
  • Data science methods

This included the conception and marking of examinations and the supervision of seminar papers and Bachelor's and Master's theses. The research strand on electronic markets and auctions draws on a further work by Ralf Peters, Elektronische Märkte — Spieltheoretische Konzeption und agentenorientierte Realisierung (Springer, ISBN 978-3-642-57508-2).

Workshops & training. I also offer talks and hands-on training on data science, NLP, and generative AI — see services — built on this teaching experience.
